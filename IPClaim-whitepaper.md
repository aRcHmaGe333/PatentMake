## Critique of the Existing Intellectual Property System

No legal entity can require that intellectual property — one’s mind’s work — requires an investment in order to be given to the world by a specific entity (person, group, organisation, et cetera). No lawmaker or law can, in their right mind, under a premise of sanity, prevent a person to share their mind’s work with the rest of the planet, for maximized mutual benefit.

Any claim of these practices being sane and beneficial is coercion into a life of falsehood and insanity. It is a forced compliance with the acknowledgement of things which are false - as things which are true, detriments as benefits, control as personal right propagation.

IPClaim stands in direct opposition to such coercive, exclusionary systems. This is not merely a technical tool - though it can be used as such. In no way does it coerce you into taking a standpoint. 

Still, its creator sees IPConfig as a principled stand for the right of every creator to share their work freely, without artificial barriers, for maximized mutual benefit. 

We reject the notion that creativity and knowledge should be fenced off by legal or economic gatekeeping. Instead, we affirm the universal right to contribute, to claim, and to correct, in good faith, for the benefit of all.

+ ## Philosophy and critique of current IP systems
+ 
+ Contemporary intellectual property regimes are largely structured around scarcity, exclusivity, and the presumption that creative work must be mediated by legal and economic gatekeepers. In many jurisdictions, meaningful participation in the IP system requires financial investment, specialist legal knowledge, or institutional backing. This effectively restricts the ability of many creators to share and protect their work on fair terms.
+ 
+ IPClaim begins from the premise that one’s mind’s work should not require prior investment, permission, or institutional endorsement in order to be offered to the world. No legal or economic barrier should prevent a person from sharing their ideas, expressions, or inventions for the maximized mutual benefit of all participants in society. Systems that condition this sharing on access to capital or legal infrastructure risk entrenching inequality and excluding exactly those voices that most need open, verifiable avenues for contribution.
+ 
+ Moreover, traditional IP frameworks can create coercive dynamics in which individuals and communities are pressured to accept harmful arrangements as legitimate: treating artificial scarcity as natural, monopolistic control as a prerequisite for creativity, and opaque, exclusionary processes as the only path to recognition. IPClaim rejects these dynamics. Instead, it advocates for transparent, evidence-based authorship claims that prioritize reality, fairness, and shared benefit over formalistic barriers and coercive economic dependencies.
+ 
+ Within this whitepaper, IPClaim is presented not only as a technical solution, but as a principled response to these systemic issues. By providing a low-barrier, cryptographically verifiable mechanism for recording and proving authorship, IPClaim aims to realign intellectual property practices with the fundamental right of individuals to share their mind’s work freely, honestly, and on terms that respect both creators and the broader public.
